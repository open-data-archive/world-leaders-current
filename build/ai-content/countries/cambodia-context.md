## Cambodia — Government System

Cambodia is a parliamentary constitutional monarchy. The King serves as the head of state in a largely ceremonial role, while the Prime Minister serves as the head of government and holds executive power. The Prime Minister is appointed by the King from the majority party in the National Assembly.

The legislature is bicameral, consisting of the National Assembly (lower house) and the Senate (upper house). Members of the National Assembly are elected through proportional representation. The constitutional framework was established following the 1993 Paris Peace Agreements.
