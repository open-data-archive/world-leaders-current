## Germany — Government System

Germany is a federal parliamentary republic. The Chancellor is the head of government and holds primary executive authority, elected by the Bundestag (Federal Parliament). The Federal President is the head of state, serving a largely ceremonial role, elected by the Federal Convention for a five-year term.

The legislature is bicameral, consisting of the Bundestag (lower house), elected by the public through a mixed-member proportional system, and the Bundesrat (upper house), composed of representatives from the 16 federal states (Lander). The Chancellor is typically the leader of the largest party or coalition in the Bundestag.
