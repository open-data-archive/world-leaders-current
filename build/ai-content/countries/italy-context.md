## Italy — Government System

Italy is a parliamentary republic. The Prime Minister (President of the Council of Ministers) is the head of government and holds executive power. The President of the Republic is the head of state, serving a largely ceremonial role with some constitutional powers, elected by Parliament in joint session for a seven-year term.

The Parliament is bicameral, consisting of the Chamber of Deputies and the Senate of the Republic. Both chambers share equal legislative power. The Prime Minister is appointed by the President and must maintain the confidence of both chambers of Parliament.
