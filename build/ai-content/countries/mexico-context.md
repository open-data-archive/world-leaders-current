## Mexico — Government System

Mexico is a presidential federal republic. The President serves as both the head of state and head of government, elected by direct popular vote for a single six-year term (sexenio) with no re-election. Executive power is vested in the President.

The Congress of the Union is bicameral, consisting of the Senate and the Chamber of Deputies. Mexico is a federation of 31 states and Mexico City, each with its own elected governor and state legislature.
