## Russia — Government System

The Russian Federation is constitutionally a semi-presidential federal republic. The President is the head of state, elected by popular vote for a six-year term, and holds broad executive powers including foreign policy, defense, and the appointment of the Prime Minister. The Prime Minister is the head of government and manages domestic policy and the cabinet.

The Federal Assembly is the bicameral legislature, consisting of the State Duma (lower house) and the Federation Council (upper house). Russia is a federation comprising 89 federal subjects, including republics, oblasts, krais, and other types of administrative units.
