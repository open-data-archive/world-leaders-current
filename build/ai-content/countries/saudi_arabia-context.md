## Saudi Arabia — Government System

Saudi Arabia is an absolute monarchy. The King serves as both the head of state and head of government, and the country's Basic Law of Governance designates the Quran and the Sunnah as the constitution. The Crown Prince is the designated successor and typically holds significant governmental responsibilities.

The Consultative Assembly (Majlis al-Shura) serves as an advisory body to the King, with members appointed by the monarch. Saudi Arabia does not have a parliament elected by popular vote at the national level. The Council of Ministers, chaired by the King, exercises executive and regulatory authority.
