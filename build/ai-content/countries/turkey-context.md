## Turkey — Government System

Turkey is a presidential republic following constitutional changes approved by referendum in 2017 and implemented after the 2018 elections. The President is both the head of state and head of government, elected by direct popular vote for a five-year term, renewable once. The office of Prime Minister was abolished under this system.

The Grand National Assembly is the unicameral legislature, with 600 members elected for five-year terms. The President appoints the Cabinet and Vice Presidents. Turkey is a unitary state divided into 81 provinces, each administered by an appointed governor.
