## United States — Government System

The United States of America is a presidential federal republic. The President serves as both the head of state and head of government, elected indirectly through the Electoral College for a four-year term, limited to two terms by the 22nd Amendment. The Vice President is elected on the same ticket.

The United States Congress is bicameral, consisting of the Senate (100 members, two per state, serving six-year terms) and the House of Representatives (435 members, apportioned by population, serving two-year terms). The federal system divides power between the national government and 50 state governments, each with its own elected governor and legislature.
