## Argentina — Government System

Argentina is a presidential federal republic. The President serves as both the head of state and head of government, elected by direct popular vote for a four-year term and eligible for one consecutive re-election. The Vice President is elected on the same ticket.

The National Congress is bicameral, consisting of the Senate and the Chamber of Deputies. Argentina is a federation of 23 provinces and the Autonomous City of Buenos Aires, each with its own governor and legislature.
