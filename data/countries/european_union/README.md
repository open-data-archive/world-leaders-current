## European Union — Governance Structure

The European Union (EU) is a supranational political and economic union of member states in Europe. It has multiple leadership positions rather than a single head of state or government. The President of the European Commission heads the executive branch, the President of the European Council chairs summits of EU heads of state and government, and the President of the European Parliament presides over the directly elected legislative body.

The EU&#39;s institutional framework includes the European Commission (executive), the Council of the European Union (representing member state governments), the European Parliament (directly elected by EU citizens), and the European Council (setting overall political direction). Decision-making involves complex interactions among these institutions, with competences shared between the EU and its member states.
