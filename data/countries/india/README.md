## India — Government System

India is a federal parliamentary republic. The Prime Minister is the head of government and holds executive authority, while the President is the head of state in a largely ceremonial capacity. The President is elected by an electoral college composed of members of both houses of Parliament and state legislatures.

The Parliament is bicameral, consisting of the Rajya Sabha (Council of States, upper house) and the Lok Sabha (House of the People, lower house). India is a union of 28 states and 8 union territories, each with varying degrees of self-governance. The Prime Minister is the leader of the majority party or coalition in the Lok Sabha.
