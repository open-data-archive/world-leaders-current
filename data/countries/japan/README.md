## Japan — Government System

Japan is a parliamentary constitutional monarchy. The Emperor is the head of state in a ceremonial role defined as the &#34;symbol of the State and of the unity of the people&#34; under the postwar constitution. The Prime Minister is the head of government, elected by the National Diet and appointed by the Emperor.

The National Diet is the bicameral legislature, consisting of the House of Representatives (lower house) and the House of Councillors (upper house). The Prime Minister is typically the leader of the majority party or coalition in the House of Representatives. Executive power is vested in the Cabinet, led by the Prime Minister.
