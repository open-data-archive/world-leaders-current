## Government System

## South Africa — Government System

South Africa is a parliamentary republic. The President serves as both the head of state and head of government, elected by the National Assembly from among its members. The President appoints the Deputy President and the Cabinet.

The Parliament is bicameral, consisting of the National Assembly (lower house) and the National Council of Provinces (upper house). South Africa is divided into nine provinces, each with its own legislature and premier. The constitution, adopted in 1996, is regarded as the supreme law of the country.
