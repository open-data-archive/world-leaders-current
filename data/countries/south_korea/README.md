## South Korea — Government System

The Republic of Korea (South Korea) is a presidential republic. The President is both the head of state and head of government, elected by direct popular vote for a single five-year term with no possibility of re-election. The Prime Minister is appointed by the President with the consent of the National Assembly and assists in executive functions.

The National Assembly is the unicameral legislature, with members serving four-year terms. The constitutional framework is based on the Sixth Republic Constitution of 1987, which established direct presidential elections and strengthened civil liberties.
