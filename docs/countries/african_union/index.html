## African Union — Governance Structure The African Union (AU) is an intergovernmental organization consisting of 55 member states across the African continent. The AU Chairperson is the ceremonial head of the organization, a role held on a rotating basis by a sitting head of state or government of a member country, typically for a one-year term. The Chairperson of the African Union Commission heads the AU's administrative and executive secretariat. The AU's principal organs include the Assembly of Heads of State and Government (supreme decision-making body), the Executive Council (composed of foreign ministers), the Pan-African Parliament, and the African Union Commission (the permanent secretariat based in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia). The organization was established in 2002 as the successor to the Organisation of African Unity.
