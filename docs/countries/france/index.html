## France — Government System France is a semi-presidential republic under the constitution of the Fifth Republic, established in 1958. The President is the head of state, elected by direct popular vote for a five-year term, and holds significant executive powers including foreign policy and defense. The Prime Minister is the head of government, appointed by the President, and is responsible to Parliament. The Parliament is bicameral, consisting of the National Assembly (lower house) and the Senate (upper house). The system allows for "cohabitation," in which the President and Prime Minister may come from different political parties.
