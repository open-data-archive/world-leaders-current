## Philippines — Government System The Philippines is a presidential republic. The President serves as both the head of state and head of government, elected by direct popular vote for a single six-year term with no possibility of re-election. The Vice President is elected separately and may serve up to two consecutive terms. The legislature, known as Congress, is bicameral, consisting of the Senate (upper house) and the House of Representatives (lower house). Senators serve six-year terms, while representatives serve three-year terms. The country is a unitary state divided into regions, provinces, cities, and municipalities.
